“As my father used to say: "There are two sure ways to lose a friend, one is to borrow, the other to lend.” ― Patrick Rothfuss, The Name of the Wind

The truth about personal loans.

Some personal loans are better than others, and before you commit to a dangerous predatory loan, it’s important to understand the facts and risks of all the different types of loans you may consider.

Types of Loans

A secured loan is a loan in which the borrower pledges some asset (e.g. a car or house) as collateral.
A mortgage loan is a very common type of loan, used by many individuals to purchase residential property.

Unsecured Loans are monetary loans that are not secured against the borrower's assets. These may be available from financial institutions under many different guises or marketing packages:

  • credit card debit
  • personal loans
  • bank overdrafts
  • credit facilities or lines of credit
  • corporate bonds
  • peer-to-peer lending
